# Top 3 Uses by Technique

What each technique does best. When combining, the result may be a new distinct thing.

---

## FM Synthesis

### 1. Bell/Chime (1:1 ratio, index 2-4)
**When to use:** Precise, crystalline moments. Pointillist compositions.
- Clean attack, no sustain, long release
- Works at any pitch
- Good for punctuation, not drone

### 2. Brass/Wind Swell (2:1 or 3:1 ratio, index 3-6)
**When to use:** Building tension, majestic moments.
- Needs breathy envelope (attack > 0.1s)
- Louder = more dramatic
- Good for arcs, crescendos

### 3. Drone Texture (1:1 or 3:2 ratio, index 0.5-2)
**When to use:** Underlying atmosphere, meditation.
- Long everything (attack, sustain, release)
- Layer multiple detuned for thickness
- Stays out of the way of visuals

**Not good for:** Percussion (use bytebeat or samples), realistic strings, bass

## Strange Attractors

### 1. The Orbit (slow rotation, long trails)
**When to use:** Contemplative viewing, meditation on chaos.
- Camera circles the attractor
- Trails accumulate into characteristic shape
- Best with 50-200 particles

### 2. Parameter Drift (changing rho/c over time)
**When to use:** Transformation, evolution, passage of time.
- Attractor morphs between regimes
- Can become turbulent or calm
- Requires precomputation of multiple trajectories

### 3. Particle Cloud (many particles, short trails)
**When to use:** Energy, chaos, swarm behavior.
- Like fireflies or neurons firing
- No clear shape, just motion
- Good for fast cuts or intense moments

**Not good for:** Sharp geometric patterns, text, predictable repetition

---

## Bytebeat

### 1. Glitch Rhythm (industrial patterns)
**When to use:** Mechanical tension, retro computing aesthetic.
- `t*(t>>12&((t>>8|t>>14)&63|t>>4))` style
- Use at 20-30% volume under other audio
- Syncs well to fast cuts

### 2. Melodic Emergence (evolving arpeggios)
**When to use:** Surprising beauty from math.
- `(t*(t>>5|t>>8))>>(t>>16)` style
- Changes over time without repeating
- Best when it's the only melodic element

### 3. Texture Layer (filtered noise)
**When to use:** Grittiness, lo-fi feel.
- Run through lowpass filter
- Very quiet, almost subliminal
- Unifies disparate visual elements

**Not good for:** Traditional harmony, clean tones, predictable composition

---

## Reaction-Diffusion

### 1. Coral Growth (F=0.0545, k=0.062)
**When to use:** Organic emergence, life-like behavior.
- Branching patterns growing outward
- Slow, mesmerizing
- Good for "origin of life" themes

### 2. Fingerprint/Zebra (F=0.037, k=0.06)
**When to use:** Order from chaos, natural patterns.
- Parallel stripes forming and breaking
- Can be used as displacement map
- Good for transitions

### 3. Living Cells (F=0.022, k=0.051)
**When to use:** Biological processes, division/creation.
- Cells divide and replicate
- Cyclical behavior
- Good for 30s+ observation

**Not good for:** Sharp edges, fast motion, geometric precision
