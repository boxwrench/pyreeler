# What the Light Kept - Audio Timeline

This timeline is for the first full-duration preview. The score should emerge from pressure and signal rather than starting as obvious music.

## Stem Model

- `ambience`: filtered hiss, low electrical rumble, distant room-tone pressure
- `pulse`: intermittent sync pulse, faint and unstable
- `glitches`: digital ticks, buffer scrapes, brief bright tears
- `voice`: dry synthetic voice with light degradation
- `score`: restrained drone and late harmonic bloom

## Mix Rules

- Prioritize voice intelligibility over score density
- Use real negative space between cues
- Keep the preview mix readable rather than over-processed
- Duck the score slightly under voice lines
- Let the rupture section feel comparatively bare and harsh

## Voice Treatment

- Slight formant shaping
- Mild vocoder texture only if it stays intelligible
- Light bit reduction
- Occasional consonant dropout during corrupted sections
- Small reverb tail introduced only in the late film so the voice becomes more ghostlike over time

## Benchmark Notes

When rendered, record:

- whether each cue remains intelligible
- whether the rupture feels meaningfully different from earlier sections
- whether the first harmonic bloom lands emotionally
